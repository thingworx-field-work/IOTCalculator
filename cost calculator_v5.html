IoT Solution Cost Calculator

This guide represents an overview of many of the resources and costs necessary to create and deploy an enterprise-grade IOT solution. Asset Monitoring and Utilization (AMU) is used for the purposes of this calculator as the target use case, to demonstrate specific requirements.

Note that your deployment may be more or less complex, depending on several factors. This guide presented as a generic overview using standard conditions.

Getting started

Follow the steps below to walk through the requirements and costs of creating your own DIY IOT solution, as compared to using a purpose-built solution like Thingworx.

Step 1: Does your team include the following roles?

Infrastructure & Architecture Great! Having someone with infrastructure and architecture expertise ensures your solution is scalable, resilient, and well-structured from the ground up. This role is essential for DIY IoT solutions—without it, you risk building a fragile system that can't scale or handle real-world demands.
Connectivity & Device Integration Excellent! A team member skilled in device integration can streamline connectivity and reduce time spent troubleshooting hardware communication. Device integration is a cornerstone of IoT—without this expertise, connecting and managing devices can become a major bottleneck.
Data Modeling & Processing Perfect! Someone who understands data modeling can help structure your data effectively and enable meaningful analytics. DIY platforms need strong data architecture—without it, your data may be inconsistent, hard to query, and difficult to scale.
Application Development Good! Application developers bring your IoT data to life through dashboards, workflows, and user interfaces. Without this role, building usable applications on top of your IoT data will be slow and potentially unusable for stakeholders.
Security & Compliance Fantastic! Security and compliance experts help protect your data and ensure your solution meets regulatory standards. IoT systems are vulnerable without proper security—this role is critical to avoid breaches and ensure trustworthiness.
Testing & QA Great! QA specialists ensure your solution works reliably across devices and conditions, catching issues before they reach users. Skipping this role in a DIY build can lead to unstable releases, bugs in production, and costly rework.
DevOps & Monitoring Excellent! DevOps ensures smooth deployment, uptime, and performance monitoring—key for any production-grade IoT system. Without DevOps, maintaining and scaling your DIY solution becomes risky and labor-intensive.
Support & Maintenance Great! Having support and maintenance capabilities means your team can respond quickly to issues and keep the system running smoothly. DIY solutions require ongoing upkeep—without this role, downtime and unresolved issues can severely impact operations.
